CAROL SCOTT

Carol Scott is an attorney with more than three decades of experience representing clients in the health care industry. She has represented for-profit, not-for-profit, district and county hospitals and health systems and academic medical centers in California and other states, as well as psychiatric hospitals; skilled nursing facilities, community care facilities and other types of residential care facilities; sober living homes, drug and alcohol treatment programs; hospices and home health agencies; nursing registries; pharmacies; infusion centers, and durable medical equipment companies; adult day health centers; dialysis centers; and clinics. She has also represented IPAs and MSOs, individual physician and physician groups, psychologists and other behavioral health providers; allied health care providers; as well as management companies, billing and consulting companies and other individuals and companies affiliated with the health care industry.

She has advised clients in all phases of operational, regulatory, compliance, and business issues, including sales and acquisitions, licensure, certification and accreditation, conversion and privatization; Medi-Cal, Medicare and other third party billing, coverage and reimbursement disputes; compliance with state and federal anti-kickback and Stark and Spier self-referral issues; as well as corporate practice issues; bylaws review and revision; credentialing; patient care issues; safe medical device reporting; medical records; antitrust issues and employment and wrongful termination issues. She has extensive experience in drafting hospital contracts, in developing templates and implementing contract management programs. She has also represented clients in transactional matters concerning purchase, sale, merger and acquisition transactions, due diligence and risk management; establishing and unwinding joint ventures; HIPAA, confidentiality, privacy and security issues; legal audits; peer review, medical staff and physician assistance issues; corporate practice; and consent and bio-medical ethics matters. She has conducted internal investigations and counseled hospitals and hospital systems on addressing government audits and investigations. She has also represented clients in overpayment disputes, RAC audits, exclusion and termination proceedings, and represented physicians, hospitals, and Boards of Directors in peer review and medical staff proceedings. She has acted as an arbitrator, mediator and hearing officer in medical staff peer review cases, contract and reimbursement disputes, and other health care matters.

Carol has served as counsel for subcommittees of the U.S. House of Representatives and as a health policy specialist with the Federal Trade Commission. She has lectured and written numerous articles on such topics as federally qualified health centers, OSHA, changes in California health care law, ethical issues in managed care, fraud and abuse, licensing, ombudsman programs, healthcare information, and privacy and confidentiality issues. She is the co-author of a book chapter on health information networks and the electronic exchange of health care information.

TEACHING

Carol has taught healthcare law and policy at Arizona State University School of Health Management and Policy, the University of Southern California Law School, and California State University, Los Angeles.

EDUCATION

Carol received her undergraduate degree in history, summa cum laude, from the University of California at Los Angeles in 1971, and her law degree from UCLA Law School in 1974. She also obtained a master's degree in health care administration from the Harvard School of Public Health in 1979 and attended the John F. Kennedy School of Government mid-career program.

COMMUNITY WORK

Our office is committed to providing pro bono legal services to individuals and nonprofit organizations. Carol has served as a member of the Ethics Resource Committee at Children’s Hospital for many years. She currently chairs the Health Law Committee of the Beverly Hills Bar Association, and is the legislation co-chair of the California Health Care Law Committee of the California State Bar.
